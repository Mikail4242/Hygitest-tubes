Specific Sorbent Tubes

Carbon Beads Tubes: Carbon beads tubes utilize activated carbon beads as the sorbent material, offering high adsorption capacity for a wide range of organic compounds. They are particularly effective for trapping volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and are commonly used in environmental monitoring and industrial hygiene assessments.

Drying Tubes: Drying tubes contain a desiccant material, such as silica gel or molecular sieves, to remove moisture from air samples. They are essential for preventing condensation and preserving the integrity of collected samples during air sampling campaigns, particularly in high humidity environments.

Firebrick Tubes: Firebrick tubes utilize a porous ceramic material, such as firebrick, as the sorbent medium. They are often employed for sampling high-temperature gases and are capable of withstanding extreme heat, making them suitable for applications such as industrial emissions testing and combustion analysis.

Florisil Tubes: Florisil tubes contain Florisil, a highly selective adsorbent material composed of magnesium silicate, which is effective for removing interfering compounds from air samples. They are commonly used in analytical chemistry applications, such as pesticide analysis and environmental monitoring, where sample purification is essential.

Molecular Sieve Tubes: Molecular sieve tubes utilize zeolite-based sorbents with uniform pore structures to selectively adsorb target compounds from air samples. They are highly effective for trapping polar and non-polar compounds and are widely used in gas chromatography applications, including environmental analysis and chemical process monitoring.

Oxidizer Tubes: Oxidizer tubes contain a chemical oxidizing agent, such as potassium permanganate or potassium dichromate, which reacts with specific compounds to facilitate their removal from air samples. They are often employed for sampling reactive gases and are essential for accurate analysis of air pollutants in industrial emissions and atmospheric monitoring.

Soda Lime Tubes: Soda lime tubes contain soda lime, a mixture of calcium hydroxide and sodium hydroxide, which effectively absorbs acidic gases, such as carbon dioxide and sulfur dioxide, from air samples. They are commonly used in applications where removal of acidic contaminants is necessary, such as indoor air quality assessments and respiratory protection testing.
